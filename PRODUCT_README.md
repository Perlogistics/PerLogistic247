# PerLogistics - Decentralized Supply Chain Protocol

## 🚀 Product Overview

PerLogistics is a revolutionary decentralized supply chain visibility and coordination protocol built on Soroban (Stellar blockchain). We provide enterprise-grade supply chain management with blockchain-backed transparency, privacy-preserving compliance verification, and automated financial settlements.

### Vision
To create the world's most trusted, transparent, and efficient global supply chain infrastructure where every shipment is verifiable, every compliance claim is provable, and every payment is automated.

### Mission
Empower supply chain stakeholders with decentralized technology that eliminates information asymmetry, reduces fraud, ensures regulatory compliance, and enables trustless collaboration across complex global logistics networks.

---

## 🎯 Problem Statement

### Critical Supply Chain Challenges

**1. Information Asymmetry & Lack of Transparency**
- 60% of supply chain disruptions are caused by lack of visibility
- Multiple stakeholders operate in silos with incompatible systems
- No single source of truth for shipment status and events
- Manual reconciliation processes take weeks

**2. Fraud and Counterfeit Products**
- Global counterfeiting market exceeds $500 billion annually
- Bill of lading fraud costs logistics industry $50B+ yearly
- Product origin claims are easily falsified
- Quality certifications can be forged

**3. Regulatory Compliance Burden**
- EU CSRD, US UFLPA, and ESG reporting requirements increasing
- Manual compliance verification is expensive and error-prone
- Audit trails are fragmented across multiple systems
- Privacy concerns limit data sharing for verification

**4. Payment Inefficiencies**
- 30-90 day payment terms strain cash flow
- Disputes over delivery/quality cause payment delays
- Cross-border payments are slow and expensive
- Lack of automated triggers for conditional payments

**5. Supply Chain Resilience Risks**
- COVID-19 exposed single points of failure
- Port concentration risks (e.g., 40% of US freight through 3 ports)
- Supplier dependency not easily mapped
- Geographic risks hard to quantify

## 💡 Solution: 5-Layer Protocol Architecture

### Layer 1: Visibility Layer
**Shared, Append-Only Event Log**
- Immutable shipment events on Soroban blockchain
- GS1 and EPCIS 2.0 compliant event types
- Cryptographic proof of data integrity
- Real-time status updates across all stakeholders
- No single point of control or failure

### Layer 2: Interoperability Standard
**Canonical Data Schema with System Adapters**
- Standardized data models for shipments, events, and entities
- ERP system adapters (SAP, Oracle, NetSuite)
- Automatic schema translation and validation
- Legacy system integration without replacement
- API-first design for easy integration

### Layer 3: ZK Attestations
**Privacy-Preserving Compliance Proofs**
- Zero-knowledge proofs for sensitive compliance data
- Verify ESG compliance without revealing proprietary information
- Product origin verification (e.g., UFLPA compliance)
- Quality and safety certification verification
- Selective disclosure capabilities

### Layer 4: Programmable Settlement
**Automated Payments on Event Verification**
- Smart contract-based payment agreements
- Multi-condition payment triggers (delivery confirmed, customs released, quality approved)
- Support for multiple currencies and payment rails
- Escrow functionality for disputed payments
- Instant cross-border settlements

### Layer 5: Resilience Graph
**Network Risk Analysis and Concentration Mapping**
- Anonymized network mapping across participants
- Port concentration risk analysis
- Supplier dependency identification
- Geographic risk overlay
- Predictive resilience metrics
